Waveforms show us the audio's amplitude over time, whereas a spectrograms show us changes in frequencies over time. I find the spectrogram much easier to read and understand than the waveform, you can follow it like a score which feels more natural to me. Because of how detailed the spectrogram is, you can see the note values very clearly. This provides a much more detailed view of the piece.
For example, in Kangaroos, the shape of the melody is very obvious in the spectrogram- we see where it rises and falls. These fast moving peaks contrast with the slow chordal/arpeggiated sections. The accuracy of the spectogram in depicting harmonic structure is particularly evident at the end of the piece. The final arpegio is clearly visable. In the waveform, we can see the changes of pace but it does not offer such as detailed a description.
