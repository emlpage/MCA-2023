See below, a matrix- left- and a plot graph- right. Tracks 0-3 are classical in genre, tracks 4-6 are rock in genre, and the last tracks 7-9 are represented as country. However, these last country pieces have been swapped out for my tracks: The Elephant, Kangaroos, and Wild Asses.

My three pieces are definitely most similar to the classical tracks 0-3 on the matrix, which is to be expected. Although, I did think they would be slightly more similar.
On the plot graph my three pieces, once again, are much more similar to the other classical tracks than the rock tracks- again, to be expected. However, I am not confident in how reliable this plot graph is as it only tracks the occurence of A and C notes. The Elephant is in the key of Eb, which features far fewer As and Cs than a Transcription a tone higher in F.


Recreated Version

The automatic transcription is very obviously different to the original. The original piece is in Eb major, however the recreation is in A major. The original piece is in 3/8 and the recreation is in 4/4. The timbre was simplified from wind octet to solo piano. Partially because of this, the notes, both in pitch and rhythm are not at all similar- although it must be said that there is still a very faint feeling of the original melody. It is almost like the tenth variation of a piece, after the artist loses inspiration and just throws notes.
I would not recommend automatic transcription as a tool for curation and analytics.
